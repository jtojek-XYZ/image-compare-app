ISO 12647-2

Delta-E Perceptual Color Check

Measure color deviation based on human vision (CIE Lab) rather than simple RGB subtraction. ISO standards define limits for proof matching.

  1. Click Add ROI Region below.
  2. Click & drag on the viewport to draw a test region.
  3. Rename the region label to identify the target.
  4. Observe Delta-E (ΔE): Pass (<2), Warn (2–4), or Fail (>4).

No active ROI regions. Click button above to draw on viewport.

Total Area Coverage (TAC) Limits

Too much ink saturated in shadows causes paper tearing, offset smudging, or wet press failure. High-quality offset is limited to 300% ink.

  1. Toggle TAC Overlay to see exceeding pixels.
  2. Set threshold slider (e.g., 300% for publication).
  3. Choose whether to evaluate the Ref (A) or Proof (B).
  4. Exceeded areas will glow in a thermal heatmap.

Area Exceeded: 0.0%

Dot Quality & Dot Gain (TVI)

Halftone rosettes spread under physical pressure, causing print darkening (TVI), while unwanted ink specs form 'scum dots' on white paper.

  1. Toggle Scum Dot Overlay to detect stray dot gain in non-ink areas (Reference under 1%, Proof above threshold). Highlighted in purple.
  2. Click Enable Sampler then click any region on the image to measure Tonal Value Increase.

Moiré Rosette FFT Analysis

Halftone screen angles must be carefully offset to avoid moiré interference. We run a 2D Fast Fourier Transform to analyze frequency rosette peaks.

  1. Click Draw FFT Sample Box.
  2. Click & drag a 128x128 block over an image pattern.
  3. Compare spectral energy spikes side-by-side.
  4. Check automated Moiré Confidence reading.

⊕ Perfect Center Check

Locates the foreground subject centroid and measures its pixel offset from the canvas center — both X and Y axes. Use after compositing to confirm your 3D product render is perfectly placed. PASS = <1% offset, WARN = 1–3%, FAIL = >3%.

Select a target and click Check to measure centroid offset.

≡ Banding Detector

Scans for flat→step→flat luminance patterns that indicate gradient quantization banding — a posterization artifact from insufficient bit-depth, heavy JPEG compression, or 8-bit render output. PASS = <0.5%, WARN = 0.5–1.5%, FAIL = >1.5%.

Select a target and click Check to scan for banding.

◎ Halo / Fringe Detector

Detects ringing and fringing around high-contrast edges — bright or dark halos caused by over-sharpening, improper compositing, or mismatched render/background antialiasing. PASS = <5%, WARN = 5–15%, FAIL = >15%.

Select a target and click Check to scan for halos.

Pro Comparison Tips

  • Synchronized Panning: Click and drag anywhere on the images to pan both simultaneously! Also supports swipes on Mac Trackpad & Magic Mouse!
